My research is in computational social science at the intersection of online social networks and well-being. I study how the mechanisms and structures of online communities can enable both personal well-being (e.g. mental health) and community well-being (e.g. productive discourse). I am predominantly interested in proactive approaches — those that are self-sustaining, self-correcting, and promote positive behaviors — rather than removing harmful behaviors after they have occurred. My work primarily uses network science, artificial intelligence, and social science.
