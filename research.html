research

My current research is in human-computer interaction (HCI) for mental health and social well-being. Historically, I have focused on social computing and online communities, while my recent interests have included embodied interaction and physical computing. Within the many research directions that I find meaningful, though, I am the most excited about sociotechnical systems that enable our health in proactive ways ‚Äî those that are self-sustaining, self-correcting, or promote positive behaviors ‚Äî rather than retrospective handling of harm.

As a PhD student, my work has been funded by the Center for Machine Learning & Health as both a 2022 Fellow and 2024 Fellow.
