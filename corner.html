animals fostered

My retirement dream is to host a 🌱 rescue and animal sanctuary. Besides my current cat Pearl and beloved dog Milo (2012-2024 🦋), I've been involved with volunteering and fostering animals for local shelters since 2019!

Here is a space for the lovely animals that I've gotten to know over the years!

Milo

The loveliest dog anyone could ever ask for! Adopted in 2019 from GPI in Seattle (an *amazing* organization) after being a rescued backyard racer in Ohio & Idaho. Passed peacefully in 2024.

Pearl

Loving, chubby, anxious, perpetually sick, cuddly, old-man-stuck-in-a-girl-cat-body! Not as angry as she looks in this photo, I just love this photo. Adopted in 2024 from HARP in Pittsburgh.

Beetle

Sibling of Raven. Just the most adorable cat I've ever seen! Had the cutest short stumpy body and warmed up quickly.

Raven

Sibling of Beetle. A bit sassy but tolerated me :) She was so beautiful, and she knew it! The much more playful and brave of her and her sibling Beetle!

Chicken

Came in matted, not potty trained, and scared of the outside. Named him Chicken since he looked like a chicken when he first came :) Loved to lick & be near you at all times. He left joyful, comfortable, potty trained, and found a home quickly once ready!

Bulbasaur (grey)

Sibling of Pichu. Very shy naturally, but brave if he sees his sibling adventure first!

Pichu (orange)

Sibling of Bulbasaur. Super playful, brave, and the leader of his siblings!

Duck

Just the funniest cat I've fostered. Got his head stuck in various objects, stood up like a human, and liked to watch basketball. So loving :-)

Esme

Loved life and super friendly. Protective of me from the moment she met me! I miss her a lot.

(panic at the) Disco

The smarter and more cautious sibling to Hayley (Williams). Took some time to warm up, then became a cuddly lovebug!

Hayley (Williams)

Sibling of Disco. Stubborn, brave, ambitious, and out to conquer the world with her little mustache.

Pichu

One of the Pokemon litter, but stayed with us the longest as the runt. Pichu was hilarious and SO TINY.

Tina

My sister's cat! Sassy, independent, and loyal to those she trusts!

Squirtle, Umbreon, Snorlax, and Pichu

Clearly delineated themselves into the (1) bigger siblings (Squirtle / Umbreon) who were adventurous, bold, and ready for adoption quickly, and (2) the smaller, more shy siblings (Snorlax / Pichu).

Snorlax.

Stared at me all day with love. Snorlax was born with fatal internal organ issues, but was well-loved in his short time and passed with love & care with myself & HARP's caring staff.

Unnamed kitty.
